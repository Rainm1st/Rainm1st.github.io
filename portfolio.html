This page documents our user research, personas, journey, design rationale, and references for Week 4+ portfolio checkpoints.

1. Problem Statement

In onsite gym sessions, coaches struggle to monitor multiple trainees simultaneously, while trainees often lack real-time guidance and structured post-session feedback. This causes inconsistent exercise quality, weak motivation continuity, and limited evidence for follow-up coaching decisions.

Human-Centric GoalRealtime GuidanceCoach-Trainee Loop

2. Personas

Persona A: Li Chen (Trainee)

Age 20 | Undergraduate | Beginner gym trainee

  • Goals: Stay in target HR zone; follow clear training tasks; keep motivation.
  • Pain Points: Delayed correction in group class; fragmented instructions; hard to review quality.
  • Behavior: Mobile-first, visual-feedback preference, accepts lightweight gamification.
  • Needs: Immediate actionable prompts and session recap with quality indicators.

Persona B: Coach Wang (Coach)

Age 30 | Fitness instructor | 6 years experience

  • Goals: Identify who needs intervention in real time; push quick strategies; keep structured logs.
  • Pain Points: Split attention in multi-user sessions; inconsistent execution quality; memory-based recap.
  • Behavior: Uses phone/tablet during class; requires low-friction dashboard operations.
  • Needs: At-a-glance panel, one-tap intervention, and evidence-based progress records.

3. User Journey (Trainee Scenario)

Scenario: A trainee completes one full onsite training session with TrainerSync.

Warm-up Starts warm-up and watches HR HR ring, zone indicator Uncertain pacing Instant prompts for low/high intensity
Main Workout Scans equipment and executes tasks QR check-in, progression UI Missed transitions Auto suggestion for next station + rest timer

Cooldown & Recap Views summary and badges Badge wall, summary panel Completion-only feedback Add quality metrics (zone stability)

4. Design Decisions Linked to Research

  • Realtime HR feedback: low cognitive load and immediate behavioral adjustment in-session.
  • Gamification (badges/level/buff): increase short-term engagement and continuity.
  • Dual-interface design: trainee execution + coach intervention supports closed feedback loop.
  • Minimal interaction cost: one-tap strategy push to fit high-pressure onsite context.

5. Evaluation Plan (Week 8 to Week 10)

  • Participants: 6-10 users (mix of trainees and at least 1-2 experienced coaches).
  • Metrics: SUS score, task success rate, task completion time, qualitative interview comments.
  • Key tasks: complete one training flow, handle one coach intervention, and review recap.
